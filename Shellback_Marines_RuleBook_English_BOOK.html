ForewordFrom the Management of Shellback Marines
Security briefing at post handover

This Rule Book has been prepared for all security personnel employed under Shellback Marines Security Agency. It is written in simple language so that every guard, regardless of educational background, can understand their duties, rights, and responsibilities.

Every security guard must read this book carefully. If you cannot read, your supervisor will explain it to you. This book is your guide to being a professional, law-abiding, and respected security officer.

Keep this book with you. Follow it every day.

Subject 1: Present Security Scenario

(a) VIP Security

VIP Security means protecting important persons — such as government officers, company directors, doctors, or any person whose safety is at special risk.

VIP escort and perimeter watch

What a security guard must know:

  • A VIP is any person given special protection due to their position, work, or threat level.
  • VIP security requires extra alertness — observe surroundings at all times and report anything unusual immediately.
  • Do not allow any unknown person to approach a VIP without proper verification.
  • Maintain a safe distance perimeter. Do not allow crowding near the VIP.
  • In case of any threat, immediately alert your supervisor and the nearest police post.
  • Never leave your VIP post without written handover to another guard.
Simple Rule: When on VIP duty — not even a moment's lapse is acceptable. Eyes open, ears alert at all times.

(b) Internal Security

Internal Security means keeping a workplace, factory, office, or site safe from threats that come from inside — such as theft by employees, misuse of assets, or workplace conflict.

  • Watch for unusual behaviour of employees — repeated late-night stays, carrying bags without checking.
  • All employees — including staff, supervisors, or managers — are subject to standard security checks. No one is exempt.
  • Any incident of internal theft, fighting, or misconduct must be reported immediately in writing.
  • Do not take sides in any internal conflict. Stay neutral and call your supervisor.
  • Maintain a daily log of anything unusual you observe on site.
Simple Rule: The threat from inside is as serious as the threat from outside. Watch everyone, be fair to all.

(c) Institutional Security

Institutional Security means protecting buildings and facilities like hospitals, schools, banks, factories, and government offices.

Hospital security post
Bank entrance control
Industrial site guard
  • Every institution has its own rules. Learn the specific rules of the site where you are posted.

Simple Rule: Learn the rules of every site you are posted at. Each building is different — adapt accordingly.

Subject 2: Role and Functioning of Private Security Agencies

(a) Fire Fighting

Know your extinguisher types

Every security guard must know basic fire safety and how to respond when a fire breaks out. Know the location of all fire extinguishers on your site and check them monthly.

Types of fire extinguishers:

  • Red (Water) — for paper, wood fires
  • Yellow (Dry Powder) — for electrical and chemical fires
  • Green (CO2) — for electrical fires

How to use a fire extinguisher — remember PASS:

P
Pull the pin
A
Aim at the base of the fire
S
Squeeze the handle
S
Sweep side to side

If fire is large — DO NOT try to fight it yourself. Shout "FIRE!", activate the alarm, call fire services (101), and evacuate people calmly. Never use a lift during a fire. Always use stairs.

Simple Rule: If fire breaks out — sound the alarm first, evacuate people, then extinguish only if small.

(b) Disaster / Emergency Management Protocol

Emergency response and evacuation — every guard must know the drill

Disasters can be natural (flood, earthquake) or man-made (fire, bomb threat, accident). Every guard must know how to respond.

Standard Emergency Response Steps:

  1. Stay calm. Do not run or shout unnecessarily.
  2. Raise the alarm — shout, blow whistle, or press the emergency button.
  3. Inform your supervisor and site in-charge immediately.
  4. Call emergency services: Police – 100 | Fire – 101 | Ambulance – 108
  5. Guide people to the nearest safe exit or assembly point.
  6. Do not allow anyone to re-enter the building until cleared by authorities.
  7. Assist injured persons only if trained — otherwise wait for medical help.
  8. Write down everything that happened in your duty register after the incident.
Simple Rule: In a disaster — do not panic. Signal, save people, call for help.

(e) Information Security

Information Security means protecting all data, documents, records, and conversations related to your site, client, or agency from being shared with unauthorised persons.

  • Do not discuss your client's details — their business, operations, layout, or security arrangements — with anyone outside the organisation.
  • Do not take photographs inside the site premises unless specifically authorised.
  • Do not share CCTV footage, entry records, visitor logs, or any register data with outside persons.
  • Do not share patrol routes, shift timings, or guard deployment details with strangers.
  • If someone tries to gather information about the site from you — report it immediately to your supervisor.
  • Keep all registers and documents in a secure place. Do not leave them unattended.
Simple Rule: What is work-related stays at work. Tell nothing to anyone outside.

(f) Access Control

ID card verification at entry
CCTV-monitored access points
  • Every entry and exit must be logged in the In-Out Register with name, time, ID number, and signature.
  • No person should enter a restricted area without proper permission.
  • Tailgating — when one person follows another through a secured door without individual verification — is strictly prohibited.
  • All vehicles must be checked at the gate — cabin, boot, and undercarriage if required.
  • For contractor workers: maintain a separate contractor register and check work permits daily.
  • After working hours, no person should be allowed inside without written authorisation from a senior officer.
Simple Rule: One ID — one entry. Tailgating must be stopped. That is your duty.

(g) Explosives and IEDs (Improvised Explosive Devices)

An IED is a homemade bomb made from available materials. These are used by anti-social and terrorist elements to cause harm.

How to identify a suspicious object:

  • Unattended bags, boxes, or packages left in public areas.
  • Wires, batteries, or unusual smell coming from an object.
  • Objects that were not there in your last round.
  • Vehicles parked at unusual places for long periods.

If you find a suspicious object: DO NOT touch, move, or try to open it. Clear the area — move everyone at least 100 metres away. Call Police: 100 immediately. Do not use mobile phones near the object. Wait for Bomb Disposal Squad.

Simple Rule: If suspicious of any object — do not touch it, clear the area, call police.

(h) Anti-Sabotage Checks (ASC)

Anti-Sabotage Checks are routine searches done to ensure no dangerous object, device, or unauthorised material has been placed inside the premises. Conduct before any VIP visit, important event, at the start of each shift, or when suspicious activity is reported.

  • Walk through the entire area methodically — room by room, floor by floor.
  • Check under tables, chairs, behind curtains, inside dustbins, and in false ceilings.
  • Check all toilets, store rooms, and maintenance areas.
  • Look for unfamiliar bags, boxes, packages, or objects.
  • Check electrical boards and junction boxes for tampering.
  • Any object that does not belong — report and do not touch.
Simple Rule: Conduct ASC with eyes wide open, check every corner. If something seems out of place — do not touch it.

(i) Security Related Equipment  &  (j) Communication Equipment

Handheld Metal Detector (HHMD)
Walkie-Talkie / Two-Way Radio
  • HHMD: Pass slowly over the body — front, back, sides, legs. If it beeps — investigate politely. Keep battery charged.
  • DFMD: One person at a time. Do not allow bypass.
  • Torch: Always carry a working torch on night duty.
  • Baton / Lathi: For self-defence and deterrence only. Using a baton without justification is a criminal offence.
  • Walkie-Talkie: Always keep it charged and on during duty. Speak clearly and slowly. Example: "Gate 1 to Control Room — over." Confirm every message received.
  • Mobile Phone: Official calls only. Keep emergency numbers saved. Do not browse social media during duty.
  • Emergency Alarm Button: Press only in genuine emergencies. False alarms are a serious offence.
Simple Rule: Keep equipment ready and use it correctly. Communicate — clearly, briefly, and relevantly. Radio on at all times.

(k) Patrolling  &  (l) Post Duties

Night patrol of the perimeter

Patrolling: Walk through the protected area at regular intervals. Use Fixed Route Patrol or Random Patrol (change timing and route to prevent predictability). Always carry torch (night), radio, and register. Check all doors, windows, locks, gates. Look for fire hazards and unauthorised persons.

Post Duty Rules:

  • Arrive at your post 10 minutes before shift starts.
  • Take proper handover. Read all notes from the previous guard.
  • Do not eat, sleep, or use phone for personal calls while on post.
  • Do not leave your post for any reason without a relief guard in place.
  • At end of shift: brief incoming guard, sign handover register, account for all keys and equipment.
Simple Rule: Patrol — eyes open, check everything, record all findings. Leaving post = breaking duty. Stay until relief arrives.

PSARA — the law that governs our work

(a) The Private Security Agencies (Regulation) Act, 2005 — PSARA

PSARA is the central law of India that controls and regulates all private security agencies. Every security agency and every guard working in India must follow this law.

Who can be a security guard: Indian citizen only | Age: 18–65 years | No criminal conviction involving moral turpitude | Medically fit | Minimum training completed.

What the agency must do: Obtain a licence from the state government | Provide training before deployment | Pay minimum wages | Provide uniform, ID card, and appointment letter | Maintain records of all guards.

What the guard must do: Carry identity card at all times | Wear approved uniform | Not carry unlicensed weapons | Not be under the influence of alcohol or drugs on duty.

Penalties: Operating without a licence: Imprisonment up to 1 year and/or fine up to Rs. 25,000. Violation of any condition: Licence may be suspended or cancelled.

Shellback Marines holds Licence No. W.B – 55/WB/PSA/2025, issued by the Government of West Bengal.

Simple Rule: PSARA is the law governing our work — licence is mandatory, training is mandatory, follow the rules.

V
Interface with Public & DepartmentsPublic Behaviour · Police Liaison · Government Departments

The Golden Rule: Be firm but polite. Strict but respectful.

Greet visitors with a respectful namaste or hello. Ask for ID politely: "Sir/Madam, may I see your ID please?" Listen to visitors' queries and guide them correctly. Assist elderly, disabled, or visually impaired persons. Do not raise your voice or argue. Do not accept gifts, money, or food from visitors.

With Police: Maintain a written record of local police station details at your post. In any emergency — call 100 immediately. Cooperate fully during inspections. File an FIR for serious incidents — theft, assault, suspicious objects — without delay.

With Fire Department: Know your nearest fire station number. Do not obstruct fire brigade personnel. Assist by clearing access routes.

With Government Inspectors: Be polite and cooperative. Direct them to the site in-charge. Never deny entry to authorised government inspectors.

Simple Rule: Give respect, earn respect. Work with government departments and police — they are partners, not adversaries.

Scope of ResponsibilitiesSite-Level Duties for All Deployed Guards
Comprehensive site security — the daily responsibility of every Shellback Marines guard

1. Frisking and Substance Control

Frisking is the physical search of a person's body and belongings to detect prohibited items. Gutka, tobacco, alcohol, and drugs create health and safety hazards on construction and industrial sites.

  • Frisking must be conducted by a guard of the same gender as the person being frisked.
  • Announce before frisking: "Sir/Madam, security check — may I proceed?"
  • Use HHMD first. Conduct physical pat-down only if the device signals.
  • Check bags, tiffins, pockets, and packages.
  • If any prohibited item is found — do not confiscate without issuing a receipt. Record item, person's name, time, and action taken.

Prohibited items — Do NOT allow inside: Gutka, tobacco, pan masala | Alcohol or any intoxicating substance | Drugs (not medically declared) | Sharp weapons or blades.

Simple Rule: Frisk with respect and by the rules. No one hides — no one is exempt.

3. ID Verification  |  4. Material Movement Control

Material movement gate check

ID Verification Steps: Ask for ID politely → Match photo with face → Check name, designation, and validity → Look for tampering → If valid, record in register → If doubtful, call supervisor.

Acceptable IDs: Aadhaar | Voter ID | Driving Licence | Passport | Company Employee ID | Contractor Worker Pass (with photo).

Material Movement: For incoming material — check Delivery Challan, Purchase Order number, count boxes, check seals, record vehicle registration. For outgoing material — check Gate Pass signed by authorised officer. No gate pass = no material leaves. This is absolute.

Simple Rule: Check challan, check gate pass, count it, record it — then let it go. Nothing moves without paperwork.

5. Safety & Housekeeping Surveillance  |  6. Discipline & Coordination

  • Ensure all workers wear required PPE (Helmet, Safety Shoes, Reflective Jacket, Gloves, Goggles) before entering the workfloor. Document PPE violations.
  • High-risk areas (excavation pits, confined spaces, elevated work zones, welding areas, electrical panels) must have barricading tape, warning signs, and physical barriers at all times.
  • Visually check the First Aid Box at the start of your shift. Report if locked, empty, or damaged.
  • Report unsafe acts (working at height without harness, removing PPE, smoking in no-smoking zone) and unsafe conditions (open manhole, spilled chemicals, exposed wires) — immediately and in writing.
  • During Audits: be in full uniform, all registers updated. Assist auditing team. Do not hide or destroy any record.
  • During Emergencies: follow the Emergency Response Plan. Control access to the emergency area. Keep entry/exit routes clear for emergency vehicles. Maintain headcount at assembly point during evacuation.
Simple Rule: Safety is your responsibility too — not just security. If you see something wrong — report it immediately. Whether audit or emergency — always ready, always correct.
