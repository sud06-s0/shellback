Overview

A large textile manufacturing unit operating in the Siliguri industrial corridor had been experiencing persistent after-hours theft of raw materials and finished goods. Previous informal security arrangements lacked structure, written accountability, or defined post instructions — leaving management exposed and unable to act decisively against repeat incidents.

Shellback Marines was engaged to conduct a full site security survey, identify perimeter vulnerabilities, and deploy a structured round-the-clock physical guarding programme — eliminating incidents and restoring operational confidence across the facility within six weeks.

Challenges & Objectives
Repeated After-Hours Theft

Raw materials and production equipment were stolen from the yard and perimeter consistently after working hours. No formal incident log existed, leaving management without evidence to act or claim insurance.

Three Critical Perimeter Gaps

An initial walkthrough revealed three unmanned breach points not covered by the previous arrangement — leaving large sections of the facility open and unmonitored throughout the night.

No Formal Reporting Regime

Previous guards operated with no written instructions, no daily occurrence reports, and no escalation protocols — making it impossible to identify patterns or demonstrate accountability to management.

Low Staff Confidence On-Site

Factory supervisors and night-shift workers reported feeling unsafe, impacting productivity and morale. A visible, disciplined security presence was needed urgently to restore confidence across the facility.

Our Solution
Full Site Security Survey
  • Comprehensive perimeter and access point vulnerability assessment
  • Three critical unguarded breach locations identified and mapped
  • High-risk zones defined: production yard, raw material store, main gate
Structured Guard Deployment
  • Static guard posts at main gate and two critical perimeter points
  • Randomised mobile patrol cycle introduced across all three shifts
  • Site-specific post standing orders and guard briefings issued Day 1
Gate Management & Loss Prevention
  • Structured entry/exit log for all personnel and vehicles
  • Material movement register for outbound goods verification
  • Daily occurrence reports introduced and filed with management
Accountability & Management Reporting
  • Weekly security briefings delivered to plant management
  • Full written incident reporting and escalation protocols established
Outcomes & Impact
Zero
Theft Incidents in 6 Weeks

All after-hours theft and perimeter breach incidents ceased entirely within six weeks — a complete turnaround from the client's situation at contract start.

3
Critical Gaps Closed

Following the six-week programme, management confirmed Shellback Marines as a permanent security partner — with scope extended to additional coverage zones.

Conclusion

This engagement demonstrates that effective industrial security does not require expensive technology — it requires the right people, properly deployed, with clear accountability. By starting with a rigorous site survey, closing the physical gaps, and establishing a culture of documented reporting, Shellback Marines eliminated a persistent theft problem that had cost the client both financially and operationally for months. The transition from informal guarding to a structured, PSA-licensed programme restored confidence across the workforce and gave management full visibility of their facility's security posture for the first time.
